Optimization strategies

Two big ideas from cloud cost research — explained without jargon. Below them, you'll see a sample dashboard (not connected to your real accounts) so you can picture how these ideas look in practice.

Longer commitments = deeper discounts

If part of your usage stays steady month after month, major providers let you "pre-buy" capacity for 1–3 years. Microsoft's public materials discuss up to about 72% lower cost compared with paying by the hour for some committed options — but only for the right workloads and terms.

Best for: stable websites, databases, or background jobs — not for experiments that might be deleted next week.

Turn off what you don't need overnight

Test and training systems often run 24 hours a day even when nobody is working. Research summaries point to large savings in non-production environments when automatic schedules switch servers off — figures around up to 65% appear in academic/industry discussions for those scenarios.

Best for: development, demos, and sandboxes — usually not for your live customer-facing site unless you truly have quiet hours.
