Cut cloud costs without cutting corners

Six practical strategies drawn from cloud cost research — explained without jargon, ranked by potential savings impact.

Commit to reserved instances

If part of your usage stays steady month after month, pre-buying capacity for 1–3 years unlocks major discounts. Microsoft's materials cite up to 72% off versus on-demand pricing for eligible workloads.

Up to 72% off Medium effort commitment
72%
max saving

Schedule overnight shutdowns

Test and dev environments often run 24/7 even when nobody is working. Auto-stopping them outside business hours can reduce non-production costs by up to 65% according to industry research.

Up to 65% off Low effort scheduling
65%
max saving

Right-size your instances

Many businesses over-provision "just in case." If your servers run at 20–40% CPU most of the time, moving to a smaller tier can halve compute costs with zero impact on performance.

Up to 50% off Low effort architecture
50%
max saving

Delete orphan resources

Old snapshots, unattached storage disks, and unused IP addresses accumulate silently. A monthly audit typically finds 5–15% of the bill going to resources nobody is using anymore.

5–15% off Low effort quick-win
15%
typical saving

Set budget alerts

All major providers offer free budget alerts. Setting an 80% threshold alert takes under 5 minutes and prevents surprise bills — the single fastest win available to any small business.

Prevents overruns Low effort quick-win
Free
to implement

Use spot / preemptible instances

Providers sell spare capacity at steep discounts (often 60–90% off on-demand). Ideal for batch jobs, rendering, or workloads that can tolerate occasional interruptions.

Up to 90% off Medium effort architecture
