Introduction & background

What is cloud computing?

Computing resources—software, storage, servers, databases—delivered over the internet instead of only on local machines. Common examples include Google Drive, Microsoft 365, and Zoom.

Service models

  • IaaS — VMs, storage, networking
  • PaaS — platforms to build and deploy apps
  • SaaS — ready-to-use apps in the browser

Why this research?

Many organisations—especially small businesses—spend more on cloud than necessary. Industry reporting (e.g. Flexera) highlights significant wasted or underutilised spend. Cost optimisation means cutting waste while keeping performance acceptable.

Aim, objectives & questions

Research aim

Identify and assess effective cloud cost optimisation practices feasible for small businesses, and understand why many struggle to control cloud spend.

Objectives

  • Describe cloud use among small businesses
  • Find main causes of excessive cloud use
  • Review vendor and academic optimisation guidance
  • Evaluate feasible, cost-effective strategies for SMEs
  • Propose practical steps owners can take

Research questions

  • Why does cloud wastage happen in small firms?
  • What optimisation strategies exist, and how effective are they?
  • Which tools and tactics fit businesses with limited IT resources?

Cloud architecture (concept)

How a small business connects to the cloud

Staff reach the provider over the internet; the provider delivers IaaS, PaaS, or SaaS as needed. A cost management layer helps control spend.

Business · Web / mobile │ Internet ▼ AWS · Azure · Google Cloud ▼ IaaS │ PaaS │ SaaS ▼ Cost layer: monitor · scale · budget · reserved · schedule

Literature themes

SME adoption

High and growing use of cloud services; drivers include lower upfront cost, remote work, and access to tools that would be expensive to own outright.

Overspending

Usage-based billing, over-provisioning, complex invoices, and shadow IT (unsanctioned subscriptions) all inflate monthly cost.

Strategies

Right-sizing, reserved instances / savings plans, automated shutdowns for non-production workloads, and vendor cost tools (e.g. AWS Compute Optimizer, Azure Advisor).

Barriers

Limited technical expertise and time; perception that cloud billing is too complex; frequently changing pricing and service catalogues.

Methodology

Framework

Saunders' research onion: positivist philosophy, deductive approach, secondary / document analysis strategy, qualitative emphasis, cross-sectional time horizon, thematic analysis of sources.

Data sources

  • Peer-reviewed articles (e.g. 2021–2025)
  • Industry reports (Flexera, Gartner, CSA)
  • AWS, Azure, Google Cloud documentation
  • Published SME cost-reduction examples

Ethics

No human participants; published sources only; accurate citation (Harvard); no fabricated statistics.
